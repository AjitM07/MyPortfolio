Proud to share that our Team Delta Devs made it to the finals at Breaking Enigma - Battle for the Iron Throne 24-Hour Hackathon by VIT ACM Student Chapter at Vishwakarma Institute Of Technology, Pune Our Problem Statement : Governments allocate budgets across healthcare, education, infrastructure, and agriculture but current processes are often manual, politically influenced, and data-blind. The result? Underfunded critical sectors and poor resource utilization.  We built BudgetOS, a financial intelligence system for government budget allocation. A model pipeline that decides where public money should go, and why. What it does: 1. Learns from historical budget and socio-economic data (GDP, population, sector trends) 2. Forecasts sector-wise funding needs via LSTM time-series projections 3. Ranks allocation priorities by predicted impact using Random Forest + Gradient Boosting 4. Flags misallocation gaps where current spend diverges from data-backed recommendations 5. Recalibrates the full distribution in real time when economic variables shift 6. Outputs explainable recommendations every budget shift comes with a rationale Stack: React · Node/Express · MongoDB · LSTM + Random Forest + Gradient Boosting · React I’m grateful to my teammates Subrav Bhande, who led the machine learning development, and Muskan Fakir who handled the frontend and UI/UX for their outstanding contributions. Everyone consistently delivered their best throughout the hackathon. For our first offline hackathon, this was a defining experience. We spent all 24 hours rearchitecting, debugging, and iterating, no shortcuts. The judges interactions alone were worth it. First offline hackathon. Definitely not the last.
